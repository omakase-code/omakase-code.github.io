About Omakase

Built by a Western student, for Western students.

Instead of a pool of options to scroll through, you submit a real application. Omakase uses a combination of structured compatibility scoring and natural language analysis to find genuine compatibility.

The idea is borrowed from the omakase dining experience. At a great omakase restaurant, you don't order. You don't scroll through a menu of options. You sit down, you trust the chef, and what arrives is something you wouldn't have chosen for yourself — but couldn't imagine not having.

That's what we're trying to build. A matching system that takes account whats important to you — your values, your lifestyle, three honest answers — and makes a definite match. No swiping. No ranking by who's most active. Just the kitchen, doing what it does.

Season Zero

What's launching now is Season Zero — a small, intentional first run before the official launch in September. It's free, it's Western-only, and it's deliberately limited. The goal is to make real pairings, learn what works, and use everything we learn to build Season One properly.

Season One follows in September — still at Western, more clubs, more members, shaped entirely by what Season Zero teaches us. Season Zero members get priority placement in Season One.

On the summer question

Pairings are revealed May 1st, right as the school year closes. Some pairs will meet before everyone leaves. Others will spend the summer getting to know each other without the noise of campus life around them. Others will arrive in September already knowing someone worth knowing.

The best introductions don't require perfect timing. They just require two people willing to see where something goes.

FAQ

Contact us at admin.omakase@gmail.com.

Who can apply? +
Season Zero is open to current students at Western University who are a part of participating student clubs — You must be 18 or older to apply.
How does the matching work? +
Omakase's matching system reads your full application — your values, your lifestyle, and your three written answers. It uses a combination of structured compatibility scoring and natural language analysis to find genuine compatibility, not just surface-level similarities. Hard filters (your stated preferences and dealbreakers) are applied first. Then the system weighs values alignment, lifestyle compatibility, and personality texture to find you a genuine connection.
Is it free? +
Yes. Season Zero is completely free. We may introduce an optional paid tier for Season One in September, but participating in a pairing will always have a free option per semester.
What if everyone leaves for summer before we meet? +
Some Season Zero pairs will meet after exams end. Others will spend the summer getting to know each other without the noise of campus life. Others will arrive in September already knowing someone worth knowing. The best introductions don't need perfect timing — they just need to happen.
Do I have to accept my pairing? +
No. When you receive your pairing reveal on May 1st, both parties have 72 hours to accept before the introduction closes. If either person declines, no contact information is shared. You are never obligated to proceed.
Who/what sees my application? +
Your application is processed by Omakase's matching system. Before any AI analysis takes place, all personally identifiable information — including your name, email address, and social media handle — is removed from your responses. The AI sees only what you think, value, and feel, never who you are. The raw data is handled exclusively by one Western student following the highest standards of personal data protection. No one outside of Omakase will ever see your application responses. Your name, email address, and social media handle, if provided, are shared only with your confirmed match after both parties accept the introduction.
Can I withdraw my application? +
Yes, at any time before the application window closes on Sunday April 26 at 11:59pm. After that point the matching process will be running. To withdraw, email admin.omakase@gmail.com.
What happens if I can't be matched? +
We aim to match every applicant. If we're unable to find a compatible pairing for you in Season Zero, we'll notify you by email before May 1st and carry you forward as a priority match for Season One in September.
Is Omakase affiliated with Western University? +
No. Omakase is an independent student-run service and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or operated under the authority of Western University.
What's coming in Season One? +
Season One launches in September 2026 — still at Western, open to more clubs and more members, shaped entirely by what Season Zero teaches us. Season Zero members get priority placement.

Safety

Your wellbeing comes first.

Omakase takes the safety of every participant seriously. This page outlines our safety commitments and what to do if something doesn't feel right.

Verified community
University email required
Every applicant must verify using a university email address. This creates a layer of accountability that open platforms don't have.
Club-rooted
Community membership driven
Applicants are members of a participating student club. You're being introduced to someone already part of your broader community.
Mutual consent
Both parties must accept
No contact information is shared until both parties explicitly accept the introduction. You are never obligated to proceed with any pairing.
Age restricted
18+ only
All applicants must confirm they are 18 or older.

Before you meet

  • Meet in a public place for your first meeting.
  • Tell a friend where you're going and who you're meeting.
  • Trust your instincts. It's always okay to leave.
  • Don't share personal details like your home address before you feel comfortable.

If something doesn't feel right

If your match behaves in a way that makes you uncomfortable — before or after meeting — please contact us immediately at admin.omakase@gmail.com. We take all reports seriously and will respond promptly.

For urgent safety concerns, contact Western's Campus Police at 519-661-3300 or call 911.

For support, Western's Student Wellness Centre is available at wellness@uwo.ca or 519-661-3030.

Omakase is not responsible for interactions that occur after a pairing is confirmed, but we are always available to help if something goes wrong. You are never alone in this.

Privacy

Your data is not for sale.

The short version

  • We collect only what we need to make a thoughtful pairing.
  • Your data is never sold and never shared with advertisers.
  • For Season Zero we do not collect photos or referrals.
  • Social media handles are optional and shared only with your confirmed match after both parties accept.
  • All application data is anonymized within 90 days of pairings being revealed.
  • You can request deletion of your data at any time by emailing admin.omakase@gmail.com.

What we collect

Contact information

First name and university email. Used to communicate with you and deliver your pairing reveal.

Demographic and background information

Year of study, program, age, gender identity, pairing preferences. Where participants express preferences about cultural background, the system respects those on both sides, meaning some pairings may be narrowed by another participant's stated preferences, just as yours may narrow theirs. We recognize that cultural background can be a meaningful consideration in a romantic relationship for many people. Removing the ability to express that preference doesn't make the underlying consideration disappear — it just makes the matching less honest.

Values, lifestyle, and preferences

Scale responses and multiple-choice answers covering religion, family, ambition, social energy, lifestyle, love language, relationship intent, and dealbreakers.

Open-ended responses

Three written answers processed by Omakase's matching system using natural language analysis.

Social media handles (optional)

Instagram handle shared only with your confirmed match after both parties accept the introduction.

How we protect it

Applications are collected via Typeform, which is SOC 2 Type II certified and GDPR-compliant. All data is accessible only to the Omakase operator. Two-factor authentication is enabled on all accounts that touch participant data.

All application data is anonymized within 90 days of pairings being revealed. We delete your identity from your application responses. We do not keep a record of who was matched with whom.

Your rights

  • Request a copy of your data at any time before it is anonymized.
  • Request corrections to your application before April 26 at 11:59pm.
  • Withdraw your application before April 26 at 11:59pm.
  • Request deletion of your data after pairings are revealed.
  • Opt out of all non-essential communications at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, email admin.omakase@gmail.com. We respond within 30 days.

Applicable law

Omakase handles personal information in accordance with Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). If you have a concern we are unable to resolve, you may contact the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada at priv.gc.ca.

Omakase is not affiliated with Western University. Western has no involvement in the collection or use of participant data.
